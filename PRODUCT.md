# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Polish families and couples planning a mountain escape to Beskid Śląski. They arrive from Booking.com, Instagram, or word-of-mouth. They're on mobile as often as desktop, in the research phase of a trip decision. They want to feel the place before they commit, not read a spec sheet.

## Product Purpose

A direct-booking micro-site for 4 vacation cottages. Exists to convert "browsing" into "booked" without a Booking.com or Airbnb middleman. The visitor has 10 seconds to feel: the place is real, it is special, and I know how to book it. Success is a reservation made directly, not a click to another platform.

## Brand Personality

Warm, unhurried, quietly confident. Three words: cisza, prawdziwość, bliskość (silence, authenticity, closeness). Voice is informal "Ty" form in Polish, natural (not translated) in English. Poetic where it counts — describing light, mist, the view. Concrete where it must be — prices, dates, capacities never buried.

## Anti-references

- Generic Polish rental site templates (OLX, nocleg.pl feel)
- WordPress holiday-park clones with carousels, badges, and "BOOK NOW" everywhere
- Over-designed Airbnb-adjacent minimalism (cold white, sans-serif-only, zero warmth)
- Mountain resort branding (rugged, sporty, adventure-heavy — wrong register)

## Design Principles

1. **Emotion before features.** Lead with how the place feels; specifications follow.
2. **Trust through specificity.** Concrete prices, real capacities, and honest copy build more trust than superlatives.
3. **Restraint is a voice.** Silence is part of the brand. The design should feel unhurried, never stimulated.
4. **Landing and booking are separate worlds.** The marketing surface is emotional; the booking flow is functional. Tokens are shared, but UI density and tone differ.
5. **Polish-native, English-natural.** Polish is the primary voice. English reads like a thoughtful translation, not a localization.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Color contrast ratios must meet AA minimums across light and dark sections. Keyboard navigability for nav, CTA buttons, and lightbox. Alt text for all images (describing scene, not filename). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — all scroll animations optional.
